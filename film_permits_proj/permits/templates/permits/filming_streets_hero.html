NYC’s busiest blocks for film permits

When a production needs exclusive use of public space, like a sidewalk or a park, it generally requires a film permit. From 2023 to 2025, NYC Open Data records {{ raw_permit_count_display|default:"—" }} approved permits. To analyze activity at the street level, each permit’s location text was split into individual blocks and matched to the city’s LION street network, resulting in {{ total_segment_count_display|default:"—" }} street segments.

Where does that activity concentrate on the street grid? The busiest segments, defined here as the top 2% ({{ highlighted_segment_count|default:"—" }} blocks with at least {{ highlight_cutoff_p98|default:"—" }} appearances each), account for about {{ highlighted_permit_share|default:"—" }}% of total street-level activity. Greenpoint and Midtown, especially around Times Square, show up repeatedly, likely reflecting nearby production studios in North Brooklyn and Midtown’s concentration of iconic streets and commercial corridors that are easier to film on.

How this was calculated

Data source: NYC Open Data film permits, 2023–2025. Street segments are ranked by permit_count, and this map shows the top 2% only.

Percentages and slider metrics are based on permit-linked street segments, not unique permit events. A single permit can link to multiple blocks.

Neighborhood labels use Census neighborhood tabulation areas and are shown here for Greenpoint and Midtown, including Times Square.
